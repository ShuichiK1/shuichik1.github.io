Final Project

For our final project, we decided to design a functional 3-dimensional Rubik's Cube on a 2-dimensional board. This was
possible through the usage of 2-dimensional array data structures and algorithms that allowed for the tranfer of
information between arrays.
To operate the program, the user can click on the black arrows to shift either a row or column, depending on which
arrows are clicked. Additionally, certain movements can result in the rotation of certain faces. All of the movements
are accurate to that of a real Rubik's Cube, it is just displayed differently!
